The plastic packaging commitment gap

16 FMCG companies pledged to reduce plastic packaging under the Ellen MacArthur Foundation Global Commitment in 2018. Six years in, the data reveals a diverging story of leaders and laggards.

Chart 1 · Treemap · Composition

Two companies carry 61% of tracked plastic

Coca-Cola (3,606kt) and PepsiCo (2,095kt) alone account for 5,701kt — more than all other 14 signatories combined. Any industry-wide progress depends disproportionately on what these two companies do.

Chart 2 · Slope chart · Before-after divergence

Signatories diverge 19 points from the global market

From the same 2018 baseline, the global market grew virgin plastic use by 13% while committed companies cut theirs by 6% — demonstrating that voluntary targets, when tracked publicly, do change behavior.

Chart 3 · Area chart · Trend over time

Post-consumer recycled content has tripled — but lags its own target

PCR content rose from 5% in 2018 to 16% in 2024, a genuine 3× improvement. However, signatories set their own average PCR target at 22% back in 2019, meaning the group currently sits 6 percentage points below the pledges they made to themselves.

Recyclability advancing — reuse nearly absent

The 2025 target for every signatory is 100% reusable, recyclable, or compostable packaging, plus an ambition that packaging is actually reused at scale. At 72% on RRC design, the group is progressing — but at 1.2% actual reusable packaging, the systemic change has barely begun.

Data-driven recommendations

Three actions for advocates and procurement teams

1

Prioritise Coca-Cola and PepsiCo engagement — immediately

These two companies control 61% of tracked volume. Coca-Cola's virgin plastic use has risen +10% vs its baseline despite a signed commitment to reduce it. Any NGO campaign or procurement screen that treats signatories as a homogeneous "responsible" group is missing the scale of this concentration problem.

2

Redirect advocacy to the reuse gap — not just recyclability

Packaging designed for recycling (RRC 72%) is progressing. But actual reusable packaging at 1.2% is essentially flat from 2018's <3%. Funded reuse pilots, deposit return scheme legislation, and time-bound reuse targets should be the next pressure point, not more recycling pledges.

3

Benchmark PCR progress against 22%, not the 5% baseline

Achieving 16% PCR looks strong against a 5% baseline. But signatories' own 2019 self-declared average target was 22% by 2025. Procurement teams and ESG rating agencies should use the 22% benchmark to score supplier progress — the current 16% figure is a 27% shortfall from the pledges companies made to themselves.
